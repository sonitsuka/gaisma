About Me

Born in Latvia, Alisa has been involved with music and dance since childhood. During her first visit to the Theater she fell in love with ballet and decided to become a ballet dancer. At the age of fifteen, she moved to Germany to study ballet at the Hochschule für Musik und Theater München. She danced at the Bavarian State Opera in Munich and the State Opera in Stuttgart.

After six years of ballet carrier, she made a decision to follow her heart and apply to the Academy of Fine Arts in Stuttgart. For the rest of her time, she invested in music production. She learned piano and violin at a young age at the music school in Latvia, guitar and producing she taught herself. She quickly developed a passion for analogue equipment, which she uses in her live sets. Her music can’t be defined within the usual genre categories, as she constantly renews and expands her repertoire. During her live performances, Alisa (also known by her artist name Gaisma in translation from Latvian means “Light”) invites the audience for an adventure with herself.

Besides her musical work, she is also engaged in visual projects. These include analogue photography, mostly behind the camera, sometimes in front of it, as well as collages and video works, which she often puts into context with her music. Symbioses of auditory and visual material emerge, in which she presents herself in an increasingly contemporary form of movement. Alisa acts as composer, cinematographer and performer in her works, which are found between choreography and improvisation.
